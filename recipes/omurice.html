Omurice (Omelette Rice)

A japanese breakfast dish that is simple to make! Omurice, or Omelette Rice, is chicken flavored fried rice wrapped in a soft thin layer of egg and topped with a savorable amount of ketchup. This western influenced dish is suitable for all ages and can satisfy your morning in a ketchup drawn heartbeat!

Ingredients

For the Ketchup Fried Rice

  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup frozen mixed vegetables (defrosted)
  • 1/8 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 servings cooked white rice
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce

For the Omelettes

  • 2 large eggs (50 g w/o shell) (divided)
  • 2 tablespoons milk (divided)
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil (divided)
  • 6 tablespoons shredded sharp cheddar cheese (or any other kind)

Directions

To make the Ketchup Fried Rice

  1. Mince the onion finely into 1/8 inch pieces.

  2. Dice the chicken into 1/2 inch pieces.

  3. Heat the oil in a large frying pan over medium heat and saute the onions until tender.

  4. Add the chicken and cook until it's no longer pink.

  5. Add the mixed vegetables and season with salt and pepper.

  6. Add the rice and use a cutting motion with your spatula to break the clumps into small pieces.

  7. Add the ketchup and soy sauce and stir to combine. Transfer the fried rice to a plate and wash the pan.

To Make the Omurice

  1. We'll make the omurice one at a time. Whisk 1 egg and 1 tablespoon milk in a small bowl.

  2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in the pan over medium-high heat. Distribute the oil to make sure the surface of the pan is coated well.

  3. When the pan is hot, pour in the egg mixture and tilt the pan to coat the entire surface with the egg. Cook the egg until the bottom has set but the top is still soft. Lower the heat to medium low.

  4. Sprinkle 3 tablespoons cheese on top, then add half of the ketchup fried rice across the middle of the omelette.

  5. Use the spatula to fold both sides of the omelette over the middle to cover the fried rice. Carefully move the omurice to the edge of the pan.

  6. Holding a plate in one hand and the pan in the other, flip the pan to transfer the omurice, seam side down, onto the plate.

  7. Repeat this process with the next omurice.
